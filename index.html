Team: Beau Lajeunesse, Zach Lemis, Samantha Samuelli

Project CURE is the largest medical supply donation based non profit that exists. Their program relies heavily on the support of regular donors, medical partners, and volunteers to keep the gears turning. However, on the original site poor navigation, repetitive content, and lack of a clear message made it difficult for potential users to complete tasks within the site. Leading to a lack of motivation to continue learning about, and engaging with the Project CURE program.

We surveyed 32 participants through the use of various social media platforms.

We discovered that people will gladly volunteer their time and money to support a cause they find important as long as they feel confident that are truly making an impact in the world.

The quickest way for an organization to lose that confidence is through misuse of funds and resources by overpaying staff and executives instead of truly helping those in need.

Jennifer has always had a passion for helping others, hence becoming a nurse. After burning the midnight oil in the industry for many years she decided she was ready to give back in other ways. LA had become too crowded, and too expensive: she was ready to see the world. Through some friends at the hospital she heard about Project C.U.R.E., a program that allows her to harness a variety of her skills to help others around the globe. Places lacking with medical support, experience and tools needed for every day care and survival. She was able to start out volunteering sorting supplies at a local warehouse, taking a week long trip to a field hospital in Uganda, and eventually becoming a full time donor to the fundraisers she cares about most.

People are passionate about supporting causes they care about. With life, work, and family it can be tough to find the time, money, and energy to act on those passions.

People are excited to help others and want to be confident that they are making a difference. With a clear message and proven results, non-profit organizations can increase their funding and manpower, in turn making a bigger impact across the world.

Through site analysis, stakeholder interviews, user testing, and countless iterations we we able to create informed decisions based on this continuous inflow of information. We went from low wireframes all he way to a well signed high-fidelity prototype by listening to our users, and building a flow that was both user friendly, but also solved the users problems.

Iterations Made:

  • Introduced multiple trolling sections to condense information
  • Added buttons to expand and condense heavy text areas
  • Built a sliding sidebar menu for easy navigation
  • Buttons/tappable areas are sized appropriately for easy use
